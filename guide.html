Game Guide

This game can be a bit confusing at times. But I'll try to explain the most frequently asked questions here. If you don't find the answer you want, get in contact with me and other players on Reddit.



Citizens


How do I get citizens?


Simply put, you earn citizens by making lots of money. You will get your first citizen when you have about a 44 billion dollar net worth. However, you'll only receive their bonus after you started a new city. So, that brings us onto our next subject.


Why should I restart/start new city?


When you do start to earn citizens, you need to prestige, or restart your city, to earn their bonus. Every citizen gives you a 2% bonus to what you earn. This means that if you have 10 upcoming citizens, and restart, you will get an extra 20 cents per dollar per citizen giving you a total of $1.2. This does not sound like a lot, but it adds up later in the game.


When should I restart/start new city?


This question is up to you, and there is not just one answer. Some prefer to restart as soon as they get a citizen, while some like to do a restart after receiving 10 citizens. Either way, you'll get 2% increase in profit for each citizen, and you get to keep your citizens when you restart.

Remember that the only thing that will follow you to a new city is the citizens themselves, and your collected coins. All upgrades will reset.


What follows me to a new city?


The only thing that follows you to a new city is the citizens themselves and your coins. Everything else resets. But, it is VERY much worth it since each one the citizens gives you a huge bonus.



City


What are approval ratings?


If you keep the approval rating high enough, you will receive a bonus. The amount of the bonus depends on how high the approval ratings are and are stated just below the ratings bar. The approval ratings ticks down during a set amount of time, and will need to be filled up after a few hours. But, remember: The bonus is only available as long as you are online in the game.



Coins


What are coins?


Coins is a separate currency that's available in the game. You can exchange those for powerful bonuses. And, the coins follow you to your next city.


How do I get coins?


You'll earn coins by purchasing buildings. One purchased building equals one coin.



Politics


How does the debates work?


Debates are a version of the card game 21, where you have to come as close to 21 as possible, without going over it. However, you still have to beat your opponent. To raise the number of votes, you can choose to “argue” about three different issues. These issues will pick a random number between their limits, and raise your votes by that number.
